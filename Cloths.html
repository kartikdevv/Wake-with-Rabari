The Rabari tribe of Gujarat is known for their unique and vibrant clothing that reflects their cultural heritage and way of life. The clothing of the Rabari tribe is not just a practical necessity but also a form of self-expression that reflects their identity and worldview.


One of the most striking features of Rabari clothing is its vivid colors. The tribe uses a wide range of colors in their clothing, including bright reds, oranges, yellows, blues, greens, and pinks. These colors are often combined in intricate patterns that are both eye-catching and beautiful.


The Rabari women are the primary creators of the tribe's clothing. They use a variety of techniques to create the intricate designs, including embroidery, mirror work, and applique. Embroidery is a particularly important skill for Rabari women, and it is passed down from generation to generation. The embroidery often features intricate patterns of flowers, birds, and animals, which are inspired by nature.

The men of the Rabari tribe also have their own distinctive clothing. They wear a dhoti, which is a long piece of cloth that is wrapped around the waist and legs. The dhoti is usually made of cotton or silk and is often black or white in colour. The men also wear a bright coloured turban, which is used to protect the head from the sun and to keep the hair clean.
